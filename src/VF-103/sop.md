# Foreword 

This document is relevant only to the 141st JTOC VF-103 “Jolly Rogers” and its students in OTC.

This document contains the standard operating procedures for the 141st JTOC VF-103 “Jolly Rogers”. These procedures will deviate from real-world counterparts for practical reasons. It is expected that every aviator understands these procedures and can follow them to a degree as is reasonably practicable.

It is understood that all members of this wing shall fly the Heatblur F-14B and F-14B(U) airframes in either a Pilot or RIO role. The A and Early A variants are not flown by 141st JTOC VF-103 in regular circumstances and information in this document may not be relevant to these aircraft.

# Contract 

This is the agreement between all flight members to maintain a high operational standard for everyone involved. Aviators refer to anyone currently operating in a Tomcat as a Pilot or RIO, regardless of rank or responsibilities. This section describes the minimum expectation for members, command and OTC students of this squadron.

# Operational Standards 

## Cold and Dark 

* If operating as multicrew do not interact with the aircraft in any way until both crewmembers are in and correctly synced up  
* Ensure aircraft is configured correctly  
  * Hardware is connected  
  * Hardware switch status matches that of the cockpit if applicable  
  * Keybindings are set and verified  
  * Aircraft has the correct and updated liveries installed and applied.  
  * Aircraft has the correct bort number and loadout as per brief  
  * Aircraft has fuel and countermeasures as per brief  
  * The correct slot is selected

* Ground power on, perform an ICS check with your crew  
* Pilot radio is set to the UHF Intraflight frequency  
* Flight-lead should tune into ATIS as soon as possible.   
* Perform a radio check over the auxiliary radio.  
  * FL: “\[Flight\] check on PRI/\[FREQ\]”  
  * Wing 1: “Two”  
  * EL: “Three”  
  * Wing 2: “Four”  
* When cleared, commence startup procedures

## Startup 

* When ready, all members of the flight should advise that they’re ready to start  
  * Wing 1: “Two’s ready”  
  * EL: “Three’s ready”  
  * Wing 2: “Four’s ready”  
* Once flight lead is ready:  
  * FL: “1, push \[next UHF channel in the ladder\], crank”  
  * All Pilots start their aircraft  
* (CV Start only) Datalink will be set to CAINS, the carrier's datalink frequency tuned in (located in the kneeboard) and powered on, once both engines are stable.  
* INS should be aligned in stored heading mode. If stored heading mode is not available, you may use normal alignment; although notify flight lead if this is the case and was not briefed accordingly.  
* It is recommended that Pilots power on their weapons and targeting pods on the ground before taxiing if possible. Otherwise, for redundancy, power on all sensors and weapons necessary for use before taxiing.  
* Configure countermeasures, radios, navigation, or anything else that has been pre-briefed.  
  * FL should perform a radio check on any new frequencies the flight is pushed to, including VHF Intraflight. To reiterate, that looks like this:  
    * FL: “\[Flight\] check \[CNL/FREQ on PRI/AUX\]”  
    * Wing 1: “Two”  
    * EL: “Three”  
    * Wing 2: “Four”  
  * FL should push the flight to ground, tower, or CTAF (usually on Uniform)   
* Standby for INS to finish alignment  
* Switch Datalink to AWACS freq immediately after INS alignment. Check your TID and ensure that your flight is visible on data-link (if applicable). If there are any issues, consult your flight lead.  
* Assuming your targeting pod is warmed up and Datalink have been powered on, configure data-link and laser code as follows:  
  * Set your laser code to 163X with X being your number in a flight; (E.G. Gypsy 1-2 would be 1632\) or the one told by the mission maker  
* Set all of your navigation lights to bright and steady, collision lights on, taxi lights on.  
  * **Ground:** All lights on prior to taxi  
  * **Carrier:** All lights off until takeoff  
* Configure the rest of your aircraft according to your preferences.

* Alert your flight lead that you are ready to taxi  
  * “2, green jet”  
  * “3, green jet”  
  * “4, green jet”  
* Flight lead can optionally call “control check”. Fully move the stick in a clockwise motion 360 degrees with the rudder fully right  
  * Do the same counterclockwise with the rudder fully left  
  * Ensure that you are watching your control surfaces the whole time to ensure they are moving freely.   
  * Once you verify your controls are moving freely, transmit your number to communicate that to the flight lead. E.G. “2”.  
* Maintain ZIPLIP from this point (no chatter) until departure. Follow your Flight Leads directives.

## Taxi 

* Flight Lead Taxi Call  
  * When ready, Flight Lead will call: “One, rolling”  
  * For human-controlled deck crew: Begin taxi only when cleared by director or marshal.  
  * For the AI deck crew: Wait for the director's taxi signal before moving.

* Taxi Clearance for Wingmen  
  * Next aircraft may commence taxi only when the following conditions are met:  
    * The preceding aircraft has cleared the taxi path ahead (nosewheel line no longer obstructed).  
    * The preceding aircraft has completed its turn onto the taxiway or deck taxi lane.  
  * If the preceding aircraft is taxiing forward (not crossing your nose), you may taxi as soon as their turn is complete.  
  * Make radio call “\[Your number\], rolling”

* Spacing & Speed  
  * Maintain minimum 6ft spacing behind aircraft ahead.  
  * Taxi no faster than 15 knots.  
  * Maintain throttles at idle after starting roll  
  * Follow deck crews directives precisely and immediately  
  * Do not overtake the aircraft ahead.

## Departure 

* Keep in mind your position on the carrier and needed time spacing if another aircraft is taking off from the nearby catapult (ex. Cat 1 and 2). Flight lead has to give their flight their takeoff order and required time between launches. \[if applicable\]  
* Check flight controls, trim, flaps and confirm your aircraft is hooked up call your number and then “set cat \[number\]” on interflight  
* If operating ashore, call “\[flight number, set\]” after lineup.

### CASE I 

* Make a clearing turn from the carrier, after takeoff, call “airborne” on interflight  
* Continue on carrier’s BRC for 7nm at 300kn, below 500ft  
* Rejoin on flight lead as soon as possible  
* Keep AUX radio on tower until command to switch is given

### CASE II 

* Make a clearing turn from the carrier, after takeoff, call “airborne” on interflight  
* Ascend and maintain 500ft for 7nm at 300kn.  
* After 7nm, intercept a 10nm arc at briefed altitude  
* Depart on assigned radial, call “\[BORT, on top at \[ALTITUDE\]\]” when above the weather  
* Call “\[BORT, popeye\]” if not above weather by 18,000ft AGL

### CASE III 

* Immediately align with BRC after takeoff, call “airborne” on interflight  
* Maintain 300kts, ascend at minimum 300fpm to intercept a minimum 1500ft at 5nm, continue climb through 7nm to briefed altitude  
* Call “\[BORT, passing 2.5\]” when ascending through 2500ft  
* At 7nm, continue climbing and intercept the 10nm arc  
* Depart on briefed departure radial at briefed altitude  
* Call “\[BORT, on top at \[ALTITUDE\]\]” when above weather  
* Call “\[BORT, popeye\]” if not above weather by 18,000ft AGL  
* Lead calls “\[FLIGHT, kilo\]” when mission ready

## FENCE 

* When it is time to fence in, the flight lead will announce “Flight, fence in”.  
* Flight will proceed with their FENCE checklist and switch master mode to AA or AG as appropriate. Set master lights switch OFF  
* Then, all aircraft will announce “\[number\], fenced in, \[fuel state in thousands of pounds\]”  
* When it is time to fence out, the flight lead will announce “Flight, fence out”.  
* Master arm, off, Cruise master mode. Set master lights switch ON  
* Then, all aircraft will announce “\[number\], fenced out, lights on, \[fuel state in thousands of pounds\]”

## Arrival 

### Ashore 

* Under normal circumstances, the flight lead, when the mission is complete, will continue on the designated route and proceed to the primary RTB as fragged. If not, the flight lead will advise the flight that they will be proceeding to one of the primary diverts, or somewhere else.  
* If not done already, the flight lead will call for the flight to rejoin or change formation  
* When this happens, get rejoined and into formation ***immediately***. By the time the flight enters the overhead break, there are almost no circumstances that the flight should not be in the briefed formation unless some sort of emergency has occurred. This prevents collisions during the break. Not being in position is something you should be making your flight lead aware of to prevent an incident.  
* The flight lead, before the overhead, will explain the interval between breaks, who will break first \[lead or trail\], and what direction, or if it’s just a straight in approach. For instance: “5 second intervals, echelon left, break right starting with trail”, Assume left overheads in echelon right with lead breaking first if nothing is said.  
* Once you cross the threshold of the ***opposite*** runway, the break will start. Do what your flight lead just told you to do in the brief.  
* If the airspace is controlled, and it is necessary, the flight lead will advise the flight as to which taxiways to take to parking. Otherwise, just follow the flight lead and park next to them.

### CASE I 

* Flight lead will call for a rejoin. If no formation is given, assume Echelon Right. Flight is expected to rejoin within 90s of this call  
* After rejoin, each member will call “Saddled” and their fuel state in thousands of pounds i.e.  
  “ *2 saddled echelon right. State 8 point 0\.* ”  
* When flight enters CCZ (50nm from MOM unless briefed otherwise), flight lead will maintain speed, altitude and heading and get in touch with Marshal. This will be directed to a human controller, if available, or using the SC comms.  
* If the SC comms menu breaks and does not check in the flight, each member will check in after being cleared by lead.  
* Descend to marshal stack altitude, and reduce speed to max-fuel-conserve (220-225 ias).  
* If weight is above 54,000lbs (safe trap) and if marshalling lower than Angels 4, fuel dump is authorised to a minimum of 4000lbs internal.  
* Flight will maintain echelon right while in marshal stack, at a 5nm orbit, offset 2.5nm abeam port of MOM. Refer to marshal stack diagram for more information  
* Lead will call “Commencing” and flight will descend at position 3\. Lead will call for wings back, and hook down at this point. Maintain Echelon right.  
* Flight will aim for a 4nm initial, at 800ft RADALT, 350 knots indicated. Tighten formation as much as comfortable at this point, maintaining alt.  
* Lead will call “KISS OFF” and enter the break. Proceed upwind of the carrier at 7–8 second intervals, not exceeding 4nm.  
* Proceed with recovery procedure.

# Checkride guide 

An outline of what is expected of student and instructor during the checkride process. This can be completed over a single or multiple sessions.

## Part 1 \- Basic Aircraft handling 

1h long non-combative flight, with shore based departure. Standard aircraft handling maneuvers carried out in a briefed airspace (without autopilot or autothrottle) before shore based or CASE I carrier recovery (students decision). Single ship flight with Instructor flying as RIO.

- [ ] Depart from airfield safely  
- [ ] Navigate to designated airspace  
- [ ] Conduct the following maneuvers:  
      - [ ] On speed level turn  
      - [ ] On speed controlled ascent  
      - [ ] On speed controlled descent  
      - [ ] On speed level flight  
      - [ ] Standard rate turn  
      - [ ] Max performance turn  
      - [ ] Max sustained turn  
      - [ ] Stall and recovery  
- [ ] RTB safely using shore or CV recovery options

## Part 2 \- Combat Training 

2h long combative flight with CASE I departure and arrival procedures. Optional AAR. 2 ship flight with Instructor flying as wingman, student takes lead.

- [ ] Navigate to AG range.   
      - [ ] Safely identify and neutralise a target using Mk-82AIR in LD config  
              
- [ ]  Navigate to AA range.   
      - [ ] Safely identify and engage a target BVR combat using Sparrow or Phoenix variant.  
              
- [ ] Navigate to AAR area.  
      - [ ] Safely refuel at least 5000lbs  
              
- [ ] RTB aboard carrier following CASE I procedures

# OTC Checklist 

Instructors should copy this into a Google Doc of their own and use it as a template. This is solely to be used as a guideline and not a method of grading. Each instructor is welcomed to use their own initiative during a checkflight and should not aim to reach all of these points. This section is a work in progress and more will be added to this list. 

Instructors note:  
Fields **marked as such** are mandatory, and a student should not graduate OTC without first achieving this. These sections can be achieved usually by a short hop involving A2G and A2A engagements and are the absolute basics necessary for mission-readiness. Instructors do not have to explicitly check each point, and competency can be shown on more than one item at a time.

Additionally, don’t ignore the other role simply because the student wishes to fly front or back seat. Having knowledge of what the other crew can see and their limitations makes you more effective. Learning both is good.
