# Scope & Applicability

## Purpose

This document outlines the Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for the
**77th Fighter Squadron "Gamblers"**, operating under
**141st JTOC**. These SOPs exist to establish common
expectations, reduce unnecessary briefing overhead, and promote safe,
effective, and enjoyable operations within **Digital Combat Simulator
(DCS)**.

These procedures are written specifically for multiplayer DCS operations
and may intentionally deviate from real‑world doctrine where
practicality, simulation limitations, or player experience require it.

SOPs are defaults. They exist to guide operations when no other
direction is given. They are not a substitute for sound judgement.

## Scope

This document is applicable to all members of the **77th
"Gamblers" squadron**, including:

- OTC (in training) pilots
- Qualified and mission-ready pilots

This document covers training requirements, communications doctrine,
ground and airborne procedures, and operational authority.

Compliance with the policies as defined in this document is expected by
all members regardless of rank.

## Applicability

This SOP applies during official missions where pilots are flying for
the squadron. It only applies to the **F-16C Viper (block 50)** and no
other aircraft, and covers all ground and airborne operations.

# Squadron identity

## Description

The **77th FS Gamblers** are a **multirole** squadron, flying the **F-16C block 50** "Viper".

The Gamblers's primary missions are:

- SEAD / DEAD (Wild Weasel operations)
- CAP
- Strike

The squadron's secondary missions may include:

- Escort
- CAS

In general, the squadron is employed early in a package, operating in
higher threat environments to sanitize airspaces of SAMs and/or airborne
interceptors, and enabling follow-on sorties to perform their missions
safely.

## Operational culture

The operational culture of the Gamblers is **relaxed but
disciplined** and applied to the benefit of safety, clarity, mission
flow, and the improvement of individual pilots. Pilots are expected to
carry the mission successfully **to the best of their abilities**.

Squadron culture aims to:

- **Never punish for mistakes**. Mistakes are learning opportunities,
and debriefs can be done in private away from the group at the request
of the pilot via a private message if need be.
- **Have regular training sessions**. They can be one-on-one or in
group, and can be full training missions or focused on specific areas.
They can be at the initiative of a member, flight lead, or squadron
leader.
- **Never have members feel judged** for their lack of knowledge,
experience, or practice. Flying missions require a lot of prior
knowledge, and it is not expected from everyone to have perfect mission
readiness. Sorties are opportunities to learn "on the job".
- **Have fun**. As a virtual fighter squadron, the aim is to have fun
  first

# Responsibilities

## Roles & Qualifications 

1.  Each **pilot** is responsible for the flight readiness of their
    aircraft, including:
    - DCS setup (including VR headset or head tracking, HOTAS setup)
    - HOTAS and other controllers
    - Airframe readiness  
      The checklists defined below will contain reminders of those
      checks to ensure the pilot is able to fly the mission as expected.

2.  **Squadron leaders** are responsible for the enforcement of the SOP,
    as well as its review in regular intervals, as well as when it has
    been revealed that the SOP does not sufficiently cover any
    particular section of the operation of the squadron and flights.
3.  The **Officer In Command (OIC)** is the highest authority over the
    squadron, \[TBD\] and oversees the **OTC training pipeline**.
4.  The **Second In Command (2IC)** \[TBD\], and additionally
    participates in the training process.
5.  **1st.** and **2nd. Lieutenants** (resp. **1LT** & **2LT**) are
    combat ready flight members
6.  **Officer Training Corps** are responsible for the Training Pipeline
    ([@otc-training-pipeline]).

## Authority & Precedence 

During operations, authority is exercised with the following hierarchy,
from lower to higher:

1.  **This SOP**---this document aims to be an exhaustive reference and
    should be followed in the absence of overriding policy from
2.  **Mission Briefing** or Tasking
3.  Tactical commander, GCI or AWACS on station if available
4.  **Flight leads**

Higher positions in hierarchy can overrule lower positions.

When **safety**, **survivability**, or the **success of the mission**
requires it, **this hierarchy can be bypassed**; however they are
situational and discretionary, and **must** **be debriefed**, as well as
incurring an amendment into this SOP document for future sorties.

# Operations

## OTC Training Pipeline

The Gamblers operate a formal **OTC training pipeline**
designed to provide pilots with practical, usable knowledge for all
major F-16C systems. They are not designed to prepare pilots for every
possibility, but ensure minimum combat readiness and familiarity to be
able to join in to official missions comfortably.

The **OIC** and **2IC** are responsible for the **OTC** process, as well
as nominating graduates.

## Standard radio setup

| Setting | Standard configuration | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Backup UHF** | Intraflight UHF **+ 200 MHz** | Join directly after battery power up |
| **Tuning** | Use of channel presets as setup by DTC |  |
| **Encryption** | **OFF** by default | See standard encryption procedure when directed ON |

## Communication doctrine

- **Clarity & brevity**: All radio transmissions must be **clear and
concise**. Brevity codes are **referred** but **not mandatory**. Plain
english communication is acceptable.
- **Reply order**: When the Flight Lead addresses the entire flight,
elements reply in **sequential order** (2 → 3 → 4). If an element is
unable to reply, the flight **waits** unless otherwise directed by the
Flight Lead.
- **Transmission discipline**: - **Avoid stepping on transmissions** (do
not transmit over another call)
- **Clear the channel** (wait 1-2 seconds after the last transmission
before keying).
- **Emergency situations**: Threat callouts, defensive maneuvers, or
  **safety-critical information** (e.g., being locked, defending
  missile) **take priority** over all other radio discipline rules.
  Repeat critical calls as necessary.

## KY-58 Encryption / Secure Communications 

- **OFF by default**: Encryption is **not used** unless directed by
  Flight Lead.

## Standard STN & Laser Codes

Datalink and STN are set according to flight and element number:

- The first 2 digits are always "16"
- The third digit is the flight number
- The fourth digit is the element number

For example, `Ares 2-3` will use Laser Code and STN of `1623`.

# Procedures

## Pre-briefing

This procedure should be done before entering the briefing of a mission,
at a minimum 15 minutes before.

- **Ensure SRS is working**. Have SRS configured, with keybinds set and
audio devices set correctly.
+ **Ensure Push-to-mute active on Discord**. Use the same keybind to
talk on SRS, such that keying the radio automatically mutes on Discord.
+ **Ensure only JTOC mods/liveries are installed**. Any other mod
installed may trigger Integrity Check, and are not supported.
+ **Update DCS/SRS** if any updates are pending.
- **Download mission data**, be it a DKS kneeboard package, individual
  kneeboard files, DTC configuration, etc.
    - In the case of DKS, perform the installation steps by extracting
      the zip file and executing the `install.bat` file.

## Pre-join

This procedure should be done before joining a server for an official
operation.

1. **Set DCS callsign**. Use the format `POSITION NAME | CALLSIGN`
    (e.g., `COL. Eclipse | Ares 1-1`).

## Pre-flight

This procedure is done once in the jet, but before starting engines.

1. **Perform full controls deflection test**. Ensure the virtual
  joystick and pedals are reacting properly to your HOTAS inputs.
1. **Look around** to check if your head tracking or VR headset properly
  tracks your movement.
1. **Select the mission DTC file** by using ground crew communications
  (F8. Ground Crew → F5. Open DTC Interface), and import the DTC file
  associated with the mission. This step can be bypassed if the DTC was
  pre-loaded on spawn through the "Load DTC" button in the Fly dialog
  box.
1. **Rearm/Refuel according to mission loadout** and select the proper
  livery as well as your assigned board number. Use the DKS preset
  loadout if provided.
  
  - Verify laser codes on laser-guided weapons (cf. [@standard-stn--laser-codes])
  - Verify fusing and arming delays

## Startup

1. **Set MAIN PWR switch to MAIN PWR**.
1. **Verify the following warning lights are on**: 
    1. ENGINE
    1. HYD/OIL PRESS
    1. ELEC SYS
    1. SEC
    1. FLCS RDY
1. **Set Backup UHF radio to BOTH**.
1. **Tune radio to VHF Intraflight + 200 MHz**.
1. **Call radio check**. "2, radio check". Wait 1 minute and try again if
no response.
1. Flight lead: **Call "Start #", where \# is the starter number**. All
other flight members then toggle the JFS at the given position.
1. **Wait until 20% engine RPM** and **SEC light off** on the warnings
panel.
1. **Move throttle to IDLE**. (RShift + Home)
1. **Wait until 60% RPM**, then **verify ENGINE and STBY GEN lights
off**.
1. **Check JFS off**. Switch should automatically return to center
position, manually turn off otherwise.
1. **Check Air Source Selector set to NORM**.
1. Once MAIN GEN light goes off, **turn on all required systems**:

    On the `AVIONICS POWER` panel (by the right hip):
    1. MMC on
    1. ST STA on
    1. MFD on
    1. UFC on
    1. GPS on[^1]

    On the `SNSR PWR` panel (by the flight stick):
    1. LEFT/RIGHT HDPT on
    1. FCR on (radar will not emit with weight-on-wheels)
    1. RDR ALT on (radar altimeter will not function with weight-on-wheels)
1. **Turn on HUD**.
1. **Check C&I knob** set to **UFC**.
1. **Begin INS Alignment**. Use STOR HDG by default. It is less accurate
but GPS is able to automatically correct INS drift. In the rare case
where mission disables GPS, perform a full alignment.[^2]
1. **Load DTC data**. On the left MFD, enter the **DTE** page, then click
**LOAD**. Each section will sequentially load data present in the DTC,
from MPD to ELINT. Once done, you should see the HSD updated with
additional information if present.
1. **Equip HMCS or NVGs** as instructed (HMCS are default).
1. **Wait until ALIGN flashes on HUD** or if RDY flashes on the DED INS
page.
1. **Set INS to NAV**.
1. **Check MVT OFF**.
1. **Verify "GPS SYSTEM" shown on DED**. You may wait up to 60 seconds.
    1. Select Time DED page 
    1. Press LIST on ICP
    1. Press 6 for TIME page
1. **Set MVT ON**.
1. **Set DLINK DED page**.
1. **Check GPS TIME OFF** as displayed on DED.
1. **Press any number on ICP**. Check DED now displays "GPS TIME: ON".
1. **Check SYNC COARSE** then **SYNC FINE**.
1. **Check TNDL on HSD**. If SMDL is displayed, press the OSB next to it
to toggle TNDL.
1. **Press Dobber Switch Right** to get to Page 2.
1. **Set Datalink Callsign**. Callsign is **First and Last letter of the
name** (e.g., `AS` for `Ares`), and **set Flight and Element number**. For
`Ares 2-3`, the Datalink Callsign should be `AS 23`.
1. **Press Dobber Switch Right** to get to Page 3.
1. **Enter all Gamblers flight STNs**. Each element's STN is standardized according to the
  *Standard STN & Laser codes* ([@standard-stn--laser-codes]).
1. **Set ownship position** under "OWN".
1. **Turn ON IFF**, set power to NORM.
1. **Uncage backup attitude & set 5° DOWN**. Red "OFF" flag should move left and hide.
1. **Set Oxygen ON, Emergency NORMAL and Diluter NORMAL**. 
    - **Confirm Pressure in the green**.
1. **Confirm Flow indicator blinks**.
1. **Turn RWR ON**.
1. **Set CMDS**: 
    1. RWR ON
    1. JMR ON (if ECM equipped)
    1. CH ON
    1. FL ON
    1. Mode selector `STBY`
    1. Set program as desired or instructed
1. **Turn HMCS ON** and **Perform alignment** if HMCS equipped. 
    1. Press LIST on ICP
    1. Press 0 (MISC page)
    1. Press RCL (HMCS page)
    1. Press Dobber Switch RIGHT
    1. Press 0 to begin Coarse alignment
    1. Align the cross on the HUD with the one on the HMD, then press Radar
    Enable Switch
    1. Wait until `ALIGN OK` appears
    1. Press 0 to save the coarse alignment
    1. Press 0 again to begin the `AZ/EL` alignment
    1. Use the Radar cursor to align the top cross with the center of the
    cross on the HUD
    1. Press 0 to save
    1. Press 0 to begin `ROLL` alignment
    1. Use the Radar cursor left/right to align the bottom cross with the HUD
    vertical cross bar
    1. Press 0 to save.
1. **Set Anti-Collision Switch to Element position** (e.g., 1-2 will choose position 2).
1. **Set Position Lights to FLASH**
1. **Set Wing/Tail and Fuselage Position Lights to BRT** (use DIM at night).
1. **Set Formation Lights to Full Bright** (Half Bright for night missions).
1. **Turn TAXI light ON**.
1.  **Notify green jet on Intraflight**.

## Taxi

Maximum straight line speed: 25 GS[^3]

Maximum corner speed: 15 GS

Maintain separation with front aircraft by placing wheels on the HUD
-2.5° Pitch line

[^1]: If parked inside a hangar, GPS will not be available as the hangar
    blocks the GPS radio signals. Acquire permission to advance out of
    the hangar before turning on GPS.

[^2]: Using a full alignment, always check that the given coordinates
    match the actual position on the map. Use LCtrl + F10 to bring up
    the map centered on your position, then LAlt + Click to create a
    coordinate dialog box at the mouse position. Then return to cockpit
    (F1) and enter the "Decimal Degrees Minutes" format into the DED.

[^3]: GS (Ground Speed) can be shown in the DED INS page.
